# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted organization. This usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that look similar to legitimate ones.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives a message that creates urgency, such as a warning about account suspension or an offer for an internship or reward. The message includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website. When the user enters their login credentials or personal information, the attacker captures this data and can misuse it.

Students and young internet users are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may respond quickly without verifying the authenticity of messages. The consequences of phishing attacks can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal or academic accounts.

Phishing attacks are successful because they exploit human behavior such as trust, curiosity, and urgency rather than technical weaknesses. This makes it important for users to stay alert and verify information before taking action online.
