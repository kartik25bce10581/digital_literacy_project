# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. This is usually done through emails, messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives a message that creates urgency, such as a warning about account suspension or an offer for an opportunity. The message includes a link that redirects to a fake website designed to look like an official one. When the user enters their login credentials or personal details, the attacker gains access to that information.

Students and young individuals are common targets because they frequently use online platforms and may respond quickly without verifying the source. The consequences of phishing attacks can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal or academic accounts.

Phishing is successful mainly because it exploits human behavior such as trust, urgency, and lack of verification rather than technical weaknesses.
